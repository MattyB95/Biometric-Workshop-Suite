# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in the Biometric Workshop Suite project a welcoming and respectful experience for everyone — whether you are a student, educator, researcher, or developer.

We are committed to making this project a harassment-free environment regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

---

## Our Standards

**Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:**

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the educational community using this project
- Showing empathy and patience, especially towards learners new to the subject

**Examples of unacceptable behaviour:**

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Personal or political attacks
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Any other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional or educational setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other communication channel associated with this project. It also applies when an individual is representing the project in public spaces.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported by:

1. Opening a [GitHub Discussion](../../discussions) marked as a concern, or
2. Using [GitHub's private vulnerability reporting](https://docs.github.com/en/code-security/security-advisories/guidance-on-reporting-and-writing/privately-reporting-a-security-vulnerability) for sensitive matters.

All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with discretion. The maintainer is committed to protecting the privacy and safety of anyone who reports an incident.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

The maintainer will follow these guidelines when determining the consequence for any action deemed in violation of this Code of Conduct:

**1. Correction** — A private written note explaining the violation and why the behaviour was inappropriate.

**2. Warning** — A formal warning with a request to change behaviour. Further violations may result in removal from the project community.

**3. Temporary ban** — A temporary suspension from contributing or participating in project spaces.

**4. Permanent ban** — Permanent removal from any form of participation in the project.
